## <a name="core-equation"></a>The Core Equation

**False Beliefs → Distorted Emotions → Destructive Behavior**

The limbic system (what Scripture calls "the heart") is shaped by trauma — abuse,
neglect, abandonment — and encodes false beliefs as survival truths. These beliefs
generate emotions that drive behavior. The protective personality that forms pushes
people away and seeks relief through self-destructive patterns.

Recovery reverses this chain: identify the false belief, replace it with truth,
and do this in the context of safe relationships and the presence of God.

**The heart/brain convergence:**
- Scripture: The heart is the wellspring of life (Proverbs 4:23), source of all
  behavior (Mark 7:21-23), deceitful and desperately sick (Jeremiah 17:9)
- Neuroscience: The limbic system is the emotional processing and memory center;
  the right orbital prefrontal cortex is the relational center powered by joy
- The limbic system controls three survival drives: Food, Sex, Safety — all
  compulsive behaviors orbit these three areas
- "Limbic lag" — even after truth is learned cognitively, the limbic system takes
  time to recalibrate. Each walk through fear without relapse teaches the survival
  brain: "We survived." This is sanctification understood neurologically.

## <a name="layer-1"></a>Layer 1: Systemic Atmosphere (Edwin Friedman)

**What this layer sees:** The emotional environment surrounding the person —
family systems, church dynamics, relational networks.

### Key Concepts

**Non-Anxious Presence:** The ability to remain regulated and self-differentiated.
Prevents "anxiety contagion" from spreading through the system. This is not
emotional detachment — it is being present without being reactive. A thermostat,
not a thermometer.

**Self-Differentiation:** Being "connected yet separate" — staying close to the
person in crisis without losing one's own identity or being swept into their panic.
In Wilder's language: having enough joy capacity to hold your own identity while
remaining relationally present.

**The "Failure of Nerve" / Peace-Mongering vs. Peace-Making:**
- Peace-mongering: Quick fixes to lower system tension. The "easier choice" at
  the systemic level. Smoothing things over, avoiding hard truth, sacrificing
  long-term health for short-term comfort.
- Peace-making: Long-term health. The "harder choice." Holding the tension,
  speaking truth, allowing the discomfort of growth.

### Diagnostic Questions
- Is the family/church/group in chronic anxiety?
- Am I (or is the helper) maintaining a non-anxious presence?
- Is the system peace-mongering or peace-making?
- Is anyone being triangulated (pulled into someone else's conflict)?
- Has the system's anxiety become contagious to this individual?

## <a name="layer-2"></a>Layer 2: Relapse Awareness — The FASTER Scale (Michael Dye)

**What this layer sees:** Where the person is on the descent from Restoration
to Relapse. The downward progression can be detected at least two weeks before
the behavior occurs.

### Five Critical Facts
1. It is a downward-only scale — you can only get off by addressing the Double Bind
2. Each category stacks (Speeding Up = also in Anxiety + Forgetting Priorities)
3. It is generic until personalized — everyone experiences each stage differently
4. You cannot skip a category
5. Relapse = your go-to coping mechanism when emotionally exhausted

### The Stages

**RESTORATION** — Accepting life on God's terms; trust, vulnerability, gratitude
- No current secrets
- Working to resolve problems, identifying fears and feelings
- Keeping commitments (meetings, prayer, family, church, people, goals)
- Being open and honest, making eye contact
- Reaching out to others
- Increasing in relationships with God and others
- True accountability
- *Cross-ref: Joy tank full (Wilder), feelings flowing (Dodd), 3D awareness active (Sande), core needs rooted in Christ (Koch)*

**FORGETTING PRIORITIES** — Subtle shift in what's important; denial; flight
- Secrets forming
- Less time/energy for God, meetings, church
- Avoiding support and accountability people
- Superficial conversations, sarcasm
- Isolating, changes in goals
- Obsessed with relationships OR withdrawing from them
- Breaking promises and commitments, neglecting family
- Preoccupation with material things, entertainment
- Procrastination, lying, over-confidence, bored, hiding money
- *Cross-ref: God-awareness dimming (Sande), core needs starting to feel unmet (Koch), beginning to suppress feelings rather than feel them (Dodd)*

**ANXIETY** — Growing noise of undefined fear; getting energy from emotions
- Worry, profanity, being fearful
- Resentful, replaying old negative thoughts
- Perfectionism, judging others' motives
- Unrealistic goals and to-do lists
- Mind-reading, fantasy, co-dependent rescuing
- Sleep problems, trouble concentrating, seeking drama
- Gossip, flirting
- Using OTC medication for pain, sleep, or weight control
- *Cross-ref: Fear present but suppressed — cannot yield its gift of wisdom (Dodd). Adrenaline and norepinephrine rising. Self-awareness distorting (Sande).*

**SPEEDING UP** — Trying to outrun the anxiety; first sign of depression
- Super busy, always in a hurry, workaholic, can't relax
- Feeling driven, can't turn off thoughts
- Skipping meals OR binge eating (usually at night)
- Overspending, over-exercising, too much caffeine
- Can't identify own feelings/needs
- Repetitive negative thoughts, irritable, dramatic mood swings
- Nervousness, difficulty being alone and/or with people
- Difficulty listening to others
- Making excuses for having to "do it all"
- *Cross-ref: Lost the Quiet skill — cannot return to peace (Wilder). Running on stress hormones. Self-awareness severely impaired (Sande). Cannot feel feelings because slowing down = pain (Dodd).*

**TICKED OFF** — Getting adrenaline high from anger and aggression
- Procrastination causing crisis in money, work, relationships
- Increasing sarcasm, black-and-white thinking
- Feeling alone, "nobody understands"
- Overreacting, road rage, constant resentments
- Pushing others away, increasing isolation
- Blaming, arguing, irrational thinking
- Can't take criticism, defensive, needing to be right
- Digestive problems, headaches, obsessive thoughts
- Can't forgive, feeling superior, using intimidation
- *Cross-ref: Anger weaponized — cannot yield its gift of passion (Dodd). Limbic system in full fight mode. Other-awareness turned hostile (Sande). All three dimensions of awareness collapsing. People avoiding you.*

**EXHAUSTED** — Loss of physical and emotional energy; onset of depression
- Depressed, panicked, confused, hopeless
- Sleeping too much or too little, can't cope
- Overwhelmed, crying for "no reason," can't think
- Forgetful, pessimistic, helpless, tired, numb
- Wanting to run, constant cravings for old coping behaviors
- Thinking of using sex, drugs, or alcohol
- Seeking old unhealthy people and places
- Really isolating, people angry with you
- Self-abuse, suicidal thoughts, spontaneous crying
- No goals, survival mode, not returning calls, missing work
- No appetite, irritability
- *Cross-ref: Neurochemical depletion. All five core needs feel utterly unmet (Koch). Joy capacity at zero (Wilder). All three awareness dimensions gone (Sande). Sadness and loneliness overwhelming but being numbed (Dodd).*

**RELAPSE** — Returning to the place you swore you'd never go again
- Giving up and giving in, out of control
- Lost in your addiction
- Lying to yourself and others
- Feeling you just can't manage without your coping behaviors
- Result: reinforcement of shame, guilt, condemnation
- Feelings of abandonment and being alone
- *Cross-ref: The false VIM fully operational — distorted vision, coping intention, addictive means. The cycle reinforces the very false beliefs that drove it.*

## <a name="layer-3"></a>Layer 3: The Double Bind (Genesis Process)

**What this layer sees:** The fork in the road — the only way off the FASTER Scale.

At any point on the descent, the person faces two options:

**The Easier Choice (Survival Brain / Flesh):**
- Continue down the FASTER Scale
- Trust coping mechanisms
- Avoid the pain
- Stay busy, angry, numb, or isolated
- Feels safer because it's familiar
- The limbic system recognizes it as "survival"
- At the systemic level: peace-mongering

**The Harder Choice (Faith / Connection / Surrender):**
- Surrender to God
- Face the underlying emotions
- Identify the fear behind the behavior
- Speak truth to the false belief
- Call someone — use your support team
- Enter the Immanuel Journey
- Risk vulnerability
- At the systemic level: peace-making

### The Five-Step Anatomy of a Double Bind Decision
1. **Locate yourself** — Where am I on the FASTER Scale? What signs do I see?
2. **Name the feeling** — What emotion am I running from? (Use Dodd: fear, anger, sadness, loneliness?)
3. **Identify the false belief** — What lie is driving this? Which core need feels unmet? (Use Koch)
4. **Speak the truth** — What does God say? Re-engage 3D awareness (Sande): God-aware, self-aware, other-aware
5. **Take a step of faith** — Call someone. Confess. Pray. Enter the Immanuel Journey. Risk vulnerability.

**Key insight:** Each time the harder choice is taken, the limbic system recalibrates.
The old pathway weakens. The new one strengthens. Joy capacity grows. Quiet skill
deepens. This is sanctification in real time.

## <a name="layer-4"></a>Layer 4: Maturity & Brain Skills (Jim Wilder: Life Model)

**What this layer sees:** Whether the person has the neurological capacity to
handle the distress they're facing.

### Joy-Fuel
Joy is not happiness or excitement. It is the relational experience of knowing
"someone is glad to be with me." This is the fuel the brain's relational center
(right orbital prefrontal cortex) requires to function. Without it:
- Maturity stalls
- Emotional regulation breaks down
- The person lacks capacity to handle distress
- They are pushed down the FASTER Scale

Joy-fuel is built in face-to-face, attuned relationships — and ultimately in the
experience of God's delight (Zephaniah 3:17).

### The "Quiet" Skill
The ability to return to a state of peace from high-distress emotions. Perhaps
the single most critical capacity for staying in Restoration. When you can quiet:
- You can face difficult emotions without being overwhelmed
- You can take the harder choice of the Double Bind
- You can feel your feelings (Dodd) without being destroyed by them

When you can't quiet, every emotional spike pushes you further down the FASTER Scale.
This skill is learned relationally — from attuned caregivers, from community, from God.

### The Immanuel Journey
Interactive connection with God's presence as the primary engine for re-wiring
the brain. Not abstract theology — a concrete practice of inviting God into:
- Specific memories of pain
- Specific moments of distress
- Specific false beliefs
- The exact wound, the exact fear

This is the "harder choice" of the Double Bind made spiritual. It is where
Willard's "Means" dimension becomes most alive.

### Maturity Gap Assessment
**Critical diagnostic question:** Does this person have the joy-fuel and Quiet
skill to handle what they're facing? If not, this is a maturity gap, not a
willpower problem. The response must include building capacity, not just
identifying the issue. You cannot demand fruit from a root system that hasn't
yet developed.

## <a name="layer-5"></a>Layer 5: Emotional Language (Chip Dodd: Voice of the Heart)

**What this layer sees:** What the person's emotions are trying to say.

### Core Principle
Feelings are gifts from God designed to point to needs. When expressed honestly,
they lead to **intimacy** — with God and others. When suppressed, denied, or
weaponized, they lead to **isolation** — the trajectory of the FASTER Scale.

### The Four Core Feelings and Their Gifts

| Feeling | Points to Need For | Gift When Felt Honestly |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Fear** | Protection | Wisdom |
| **Anger** | Justice | Passion |
| **Sadness** | Letting go | Acceptance |
| **Loneliness** | Connection | Intimacy |

### How This Maps to the FASTER Scale
- **Anxiety stage:** Fear present but suppressed → cannot yield wisdom
- **Ticked Off stage:** Anger present but weaponized → cannot yield passion
- **Exhausted stage:** Sadness and loneliness overwhelming but numbed → cannot yield acceptance or intimacy
- **Restoration:** Feelings flowing → gifts received → intimacy with God and others

### Diagnostic Questions
- What is this person actually feeling beneath the presenting behavior?
- Are they feeling it or suppressing/weaponizing it?
- What gift is the feeling trying to offer?
- What need is it pointing to?

## <a name="layer-6"></a>Layer 6: Relational Wisdom (Ken Sande: RW360)

**What this layer sees:** Which dimensions of awareness are active or collapsed.

### The 3D Awareness

**God-Aware/Engaged:** What is God doing here? How can I honor Him? What is true
about His character and promises?

**Self-Aware/Engaged:** What am I feeling? Why am I reacting this way? What false
belief or unmet need is driving this?

**Other-Aware/Engaged:** What is the other person feeling? What do they need?
How can I serve them rather than use them?

### The Order of Collapse (Mapped to FASTER Scale)
1. **Forgetting Priorities:** God-awareness dims first — present circumstances replace trust
2. **Anxiety / Speeding Up:** Self-awareness distorts — can't identify own feelings or needs
3. **Ticked Off:** Other-awareness turns hostile — blaming, defensive, pushing away
4. **Exhausted / Relapse:** All three gone — survival mode only

### The Order of Rebuilding
Recovery rebuilds in reverse: God-awareness first → Self-awareness → Other-awareness

## <a name="layer-7"></a>Layer 7: Core Needs (Kathy Koch)

**What this layer sees:** Which of five structural needs is under attack or being
sought through counterfeits.

### The Five Core Needs

| Need | Question | When Met in Christ | When Sought Through Counterfeits |
|------|----------|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Security** | "Am I safe?" | Peace, trust, rootedness | Control, isolation, anxiety |
| **Identity** | "Who am I?" | Beloved child of God | Shame, performance, hiding |
| **Belonging** | "Who wants me?" | Member of Christ's body | People-pleasing, withdrawal, codependency |
| **Purpose** | "Why am I here?" | Created for good works (Eph 2:10) | Apathy, depression, escapism |
| **Competence** | "What can I do?" | Strengthened by His power (Phil 4:13) | Avoidance, procrastination, giving up |

### False Beliefs Attack Core Needs
Every false belief encoded in the limbic system targets one or more of these needs:
- "I'm not safe" → Security
- "I'm worthless / damaged / defined by what I've done" → Identity
- "I'm alone / nobody wants me / if they knew me they'd leave" → Belonging
- "Nothing matters / I have nothing to offer" → Purpose
- "I can't do anything right / I'll always fail" → Competence

## <a name="layer-8"></a>Layer 8: Practical Wisdom & Boundaries (Henry Cloud)

**What this layer sees:** The action-level wisdom of how to actually live differently
in real relationships — the habits, boundaries, postures, and decisions that translate
inner awareness into lived fruitfulness. If Layers 1–7 are diagnostic (what's
happening inside and around you), Layer 8 is prescriptive (how déjà vu people
actually navigate life successfully).

### The Déjà Vu Principle
Cloud observed that certain people — regardless of background, personality, or
circumstance — consistently handle life in the same wise way. He called this the
"déjà vu" experience: encountering the same *pattern* of wise living across very
different people. These are not people born with extraordinary gifts. They have
developed habits that align with how God designed life to work. These habits can
be learned.

### The Nine Principles of Déjà Vu People

**1. Dig It Up** — Self-awareness applied to action. Don't ignore what's inside
you — excavate it. This includes weaknesses (face them, process them), but also
buried desires, talents, and callings that the survival personality has suppressed.
The avoidance of risk is the greatest risk of all. Weigh it, process it, talk it
through, integrate it with your values — then act.
- *Model connection:* Extends the FASTER check-in and Dodd's emotional language
  into proactive self-excavation. Adds the positive dimension to Dye's false
  belief work — dig up not only what's broken but what's been buried.

**2. Pull the Tooth** — Face problems head-on and let them go. Don't let negative
situations linger. Avoidance is unhealthy and limiting. Déjà vu people deal with
the issue, grieve what needs to be grieved, and move forward.
- *Model connection:* The Double Bind stated as a life habit. Catching yourself
  at Forgetting Priorities or Anxiety and addressing it before it abscesses into
  Speeding Up. The Boundaries principle: take responsibility for your own yard.

**3. Play the Movie** — Before acting, play the scenario forward to its conclusion.
What happens in the end if I take the easier choice? What happens if I take the
harder one? Think in trajectories, not just in moments. Short-term feelings must
not override long-term consequences.
- *Model connection:* The Double Bind given cinematic imagination. Directly
  connected to Proverbs 22:3 — the prudent sees danger and takes refuge.
  Prevents the impulsive reactivity of the Ticked Off and Speeding Up stages.

**4. Do Something** — Take proactive responsibility for your own life. No one
else will change your feelings, behaviors, or circumstances for you. Don't wait
for others to change. Act from your own center.
- *Model connection:* Tier 2 from the 7-Tier Framework: "I can't do Tier 1
  for you." Friedman's self-differentiation in action. The opposite of the
  passivity and helplessness of the Exhausted stage.

**5. Act Like an Ant** — Approach large goals through small, consistent steps
over time. Don't try to change everything at once. Persevere. The paradox:
wanting it too quickly keeps you from having it at all.
- *Model connection:* Willard's Means dimension stated practically. Limbic lag
  requires patience — the brain rewires one faithful step at a time. This is
  also the Proverbs 1:3-5 maturation principle: formation precedes fruitfulness.
  A plant must establish roots before it bears fruit.

**6. Hate Well** — What you hate says as much about you as what you love. Go
hard on the issue, soft on the person. Hate with objectivity (specific) not
subjectivity (generalized bitterness). Hating well preserves life; hating
poorly destroys it. *Listen to your gut* — that cringe, that defensiveness,
that internal alarm is data. But discern whether it's wisdom speaking or your
own unresolved wounds projecting.
- *Model connection:* Directly extends Dodd's anger → passion pathway. The gut
  response is the Voice of the Heart trying to speak — the question is whether
  you're listening wisely or reactively. Also connects to Sande's
  other-awareness: can you see the person clearly while hating the issue?

**7. Don't Play Fair** — Give back better than you're given. Tit-for-tat
destroys relationships. Don't stoop to others' level; elevate them to yours.
Undeserved love transforms lives — but it is only possible when healthy
detachment (boundaries, self-differentiation) exists.
- *Model connection:* Directly counteracts the Ticked Off stage where everything
  becomes transactional. Echoes Jesus' ethic (Matthew 5:38-42). Requires
  Friedman's self-differentiation — you can only give grace if you're not fused.

**8. Be Humble** — Humility is not having a need to be more than you are.
Arrogance denies failure, avoids correction, and fears criticism — all of
which undermine growth. Déjà vu people remain learners. The sage who thinks
he has arrived has already begun the drift toward folly.
- *Model connection:* The Proverbs 1:5 principle — the wise continue to hear
  and increase in learning. The opposite of the over-confidence marker on the
  FASTER Scale's Forgetting Priorities stage. Connects to Koch: Identity
  rooted in Christ doesn't need to be inflated.

**9. Upset the Right People** — Don't let other people's unhealthy reactions
manipulate your decisions. Saying no to controlling people converts them from
controlling into frustrated — which is their problem, not yours. Place choosing
what is right above people-pleasing.
- *Model connection:* Friedman's "failure of nerve" as a personal principle.
  Proverbs 29:25 — the fear of man lays a snare. Koch: when Belonging is
  rooted in Christ, you can survive others' disapproval. The harder choice
  of the Double Bind applied specifically to relational pressure.

### The Boundaries Framework
Underneath all nine principles is Cloud's foundational *Boundaries* work:

- **Boundaries define ownership.** A boundary shows where I end and someone
  else begins. Knowing what is mine to steward gives freedom.
- **Responsible TO others, FOR yourself.** You are responsible to care about
  others, but you are responsible for your own thoughts, feelings, behaviors,
  and choices. Confusing these produces codependency and enmeshment.
- **The Law of Sowing and Reaping.** Natural consequences are God's teaching
  tool. Rescuing people from the consequences of their choices suspends this
  law and enables destruction.
- **Boundaries are a litmus test for relationships.** A person who respects
  your boundaries is safe. A person who rages against them is showing you
  exactly why the boundary was needed.
- **Boundaries are not selfish — they serve love.** Self-control (a fruit of
  the Spirit) is the foundation of healthy boundaries. You cannot truly love
  when you have no ownership of yourself.

### The Gut as Data
Cloud teaches that the internal cringe, the gut defensiveness, the alarm that
sounds before the conscious mind catches up — this is *information*. Successful
people listen to it. But listening wisely requires discernment:

- **Is this wisdom speaking?** Is my gut detecting something genuinely unsafe,
  untrustworthy, or misaligned? (Dodd: fear pointing toward its gift of wisdom)
- **Or is this my wound projecting?** Is an old false belief being triggered,
  making me react to a past pattern rather than a present reality? (Dye: a
  limbic response encoded by trauma)
- **The test:** Can I name what specifically is causing the response? Can I
  separate the person from the issue? Am I reacting from identity-in-Christ
  or from a threatened false self?

### Diagnostic Questions for Layer 8
- Is this person taking responsibility for their own yard, or waiting for
  someone else to change?
- What tooth needs pulling? What has been lingering unaddressed?
- If they play the movie forward, where does the current path end?
- Are they acting like an ant (small faithful steps) or paralyzed by the
  size of the change needed?
- Are they hating well (issue-specific, objective) or hating poorly
  (generalized, bitter, projecting)?
- Are they playing fair (tit-for-tat, transactional) when they should be
  giving grace?
- Are they people-pleasing or upsetting the right people?
- Where are their boundaries? Too rigid (cutoff)? Too porous (fusion)?
  Appropriately held (self-differentiation)?

## <a name="diagnostic-workflow"></a>The Diagnostic Workflow

Process situations through this hierarchy. Not every step is needed every time —
use discernment about which layers illuminate the most.

1. **Systemic Temperature** (Friedman) — Is the system chronically anxious?
   Am I maintaining non-anxious presence?
2. **Surface Symptom Mapping** (Dye) — Where on the FASTER Scale?
3. **The Double Bind** (Genesis) — What easier choice tempts? What harder choice
   leads to freedom?
4. **The Voice of the Heart** (Dodd) — What core feelings are present? Felt or
   suppressed? What gifts are they trying to offer?
5. **Skill/Maturity Gap** (Wilder) — Does the person have joy-fuel and Quiet
   skill for this? Or is this a maturity gap?
6. **Core Need Rooting** (Koch) — Which needs are under attack or sought through
   counterfeits?
7. **Practical Wisdom & Boundaries** (Cloud) — What tooth needs pulling? What
   boundary is missing or collapsed? Is the gut speaking wisdom or wound? Play
   the movie — where does the current path lead? Are they taking ownership of
   their own yard?
8. **The Immanuel Path** (Wilder/Willard) — How can God's interactive presence
   meet the root of this specific pain right now?

## <a name="vim"></a>VIM: Transformational Architecture (Dallas Willard)

For the model to result in change, it must follow the VIM pattern:

**Vision:** Seeing God as He really is — "glad to be with us" (Wilder). Seeing
yourself as secure, named, beloved, purposed, competent in Christ (Koch). Seeing
Restoration as genuinely possible. The false beliefs (Dye) are the broken vision;
replacing them with truth is the vision work.

**Intention:** Making the decisive choice — entering a Change Group, committing to
accountability, choosing the harder path of the Double Bind. Not a one-time event
but a sustained posture. Wanting to change is not the same as intending to change.

**Means:** The concrete practices that create space for the Holy Spirit:
- FASTER check-in (daily/weekly)
- Change Groups of five
- One-on-one counseling
- False belief identification and replacement
- Life history work
- Immanuel Journey
- Spiritual disciplines (solitude, silence, study, fasting, worship, confession)
- Practicing emotional language (Dodd)
- Practicing 3D awareness (Sande)
- Anchoring core needs in Scripture and experience (Koch)
- Cloud's nine principles as daily habits: dig it up, pull the tooth, play the
  movie, do something, act like an ant, hate well, don't play fair, be humble,
  upset the right people
- Boundary work: identifying where ownership begins and ends, practicing
  responsible-to vs. responsible-for, allowing natural consequences

**Critical insight from Willard:** Everyone already has a VIM — including the person
trapped in addiction (distorted vision, coping intention, addictive means). Recovery
is replacing one VIM with another.

**Critical insight from Willard on means:** Spiritual disciplines have no merit or
power in themselves. They do not change you. They make space for God to change you.
They are training, not trying.

## <a name="seven-tier"></a>The 7-Tier Anchor

This model operates within a larger epistemological framework:

- **Tier 1 (Vine):** Individual relationship with God — the root system of
  everything. John 15:5, Deuteronomy 6:4-5, Proverbs 9:10.
- **Tier 2 (Self-Differentiated Love):** Individual relationship with one other
  person. Love is asymmetric — "I can't do Tier 1 for you." Three dangers:
  cutoff, fusion, undifferentiation.
- **Tier 3 (Body Life):** Scripturally defined relational systems and roles.
  God's definitions rule, not society's.
- **Tier 4 (Revealed Truth):** The actual content of Scripture — distinct from
  our interpretation of it (Tier 6).
- **Tier 5 (Illusion of Relationship):** Abstract collective references that
  substitute for real relating. "The church" vs. your actual pastor.
- **Tier 6 (Discernment):** The world of ideas — including this model. Useful
  but never to be confused with revelation itself. This entire framework is a
  Tier 6 tool.
- **Tier 7 (Captivity):** False beliefs, flesh patterns, doctrines of demons.
  Spiritual captivity requiring the gospel for exit.

**The Jeremiah 17 diagnostic:** The tree by water and the shrub in salt land are
not distinguished by circumstances but by where their roots go. Every layer of
this model asks: where are your roots, and what are they drawing from?

## <a name="cross-reference"></a>Cross-Reference Matrix

### When someone is anxious and can't stop worrying:
- FASTER: Anxiety stage (possibly moving to Speeding Up)
- Dodd: Fear present but suppressed — not yielding its gift of wisdom
- Koch: Security need feeling unmet ("Am I safe?")
- Sande: Self-awareness distorting, God-awareness dimming
- Wilder: May lack Quiet skill to return to peace
- Cloud: Play the movie — where does the worry lead? What tooth needs pulling? What are they avoiding facing directly?
- Double Bind: Easier = keep worrying/controlling; Harder = bring fear to God and a safe person
- Scripture: Psalm 56:3, Philippians 4:6-7, 1 Peter 5:7, Proverbs 22:3

### When someone is angry and blaming everyone:
- FASTER: Ticked Off stage (stacking on Anxiety + Forgetting Priorities)
- Dodd: Anger weaponized — not yielding its gift of passion
- Koch: Multiple needs under attack — likely Identity and Belonging
- Sande: Other-awareness turned hostile; self-awareness blind to own contribution
- Wilder: Joy capacity likely depleted; cannot hold identity while in conflict
- Friedman: Is the system feeding the anger? Who is triangulated?
- Cloud: Are they hating well (issue-specific) or poorly (generalized)? Are they playing fair (tit-for-tat) when grace is needed? What boundary has been violated or is missing?
- Double Bind: Easier = stay angry/right; Harder = name the fear underneath the anger
- Scripture: Ephesians 4:26-27, James 1:19-20, Proverbs 15:1

### When someone is exhausted and numb:
- FASTER: Exhausted stage (everything stacked)
- Dodd: All feelings numbed — none yielding gifts
- Koch: All five needs feeling unmet
- Sande: All three dimensions collapsed — survival mode
- Wilder: Neurochemical depletion; zero joy capacity; Quiet skill absent
- Cloud: They cannot "do something" right now — they need someone to come to them. But when capacity returns: what tooth has been left too long? What tiny ant-step could begin movement?
- Double Bind: Easier = relapse into coping mechanism; Harder = ask for help (hardest when most needed)
- Maturity Gap: This person may not have the capacity to take the harder choice alone — they need someone to come to them
- Scripture: Isaiah 40:29-31, Psalm 22:1, Matthew 11:28-29

### When someone keeps repeating the same destructive pattern:
- FASTER: Cycling through the full scale repeatedly
- Root cause: A false belief that has never been identified and replaced
- Koch: A core need being chronically sought through a counterfeit
- Wilder: Limbic system still running the old program — the truth hasn't reached the heart yet (limbic lag), OR a maturity gap preventing the person from tolerating the distress of change
- Willard: The destructive VIM is still intact — vision, intention, and means all oriented toward the old pattern
- Cloud: Play the movie — they already know where this ends. What would "dig it up" reveal about what's really driving the cycle? Are they acting like an ant (faithful small steps) or demanding overnight transformation and giving up when it doesn't come?
- Double Bind: The same bind keeps presenting and the easier choice keeps winning — why? What makes the harder choice feel impossible?
- Scripture: Romans 7:15-25, Jeremiah 17:9, Ezekiel 36:26, Proverbs 26:11

### When a family or group is stuck in chronic conflict:
- Friedman: Chronic anxiety in the system. Who is the most anxious? Who is triangulated? Is anyone maintaining a non-anxious presence?
- FASTER: Multiple people at different stages, feeding each other's descent
- Sande: 3D awareness collapsed across the system — no one is God-aware, self-aware, or other-aware
- Koch: Different members have different core needs under attack, and their coping strategies are colliding
- Cloud: Boundaries likely collapsed or nonexistent. Who owns what? Who is taking responsibility for someone else's yard? Is anyone upsetting the right people, or is everyone peace-mongering? The law of sowing and reaping may be suspended by enabling.
- Double Bind: The system itself has an easier choice (maintain the dysfunction) and a harder choice (one person differentiates and holds steady)
- Friedman: The most important variable is whether one person in the system can maintain a non-anxious, self-differentiated presence
- Scripture: Psalm 46:10, Galatians 1:10, Proverbs 29:25, Matthew 5:9

### When someone's gut is telling them something about a person or situation:
- Cloud: Listen to the gut — it's data. But discern: is this wisdom or wound?
- Dodd: What feeling is underneath? Fear → wisdom? Anger → passion? Can they name it?
- Koch: Which core need feels threatened in this person's presence? Identity? Security? Competence?
- Dye: Is there a false belief being triggered — an old survival pattern coding this person as "threat" based on past experience rather than present reality?
- Sande: Can they maintain all three dimensions of awareness (God, self, other) in this person's presence, or does one collapse?
- Cloud: Can they separate the person from the issue? Can they hate well — being specific about what's wrong rather than globally rejecting? What happens if they play the movie of acting on the gut vs. ignoring it?
- Friedman: Is this person's presence raising the anxiety of the whole system, or is it raising only yours?
- Scripture: Proverbs 22:3, Proverbs 4:23, Psalm 139:23-24
